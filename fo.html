Journey of Faith

A Life Poured Out
for India

1908
The Pentecostal Fire
Rev. Cook and his wife Anna receive the Pentecostal experience and the anointing of the Holy Spirit at the Upper Room missions in Los Angeles, California — igniting a calling that would carry them to India.
1913
Arrival in Kerala
Cook arrives in Kottarakkara, taking over the mission works from his predecessor. He establishes his base and begins the work of building Pentecostal ministry across South India.
January 1914
First Pentecostal Church in India
After a four-day revival, Cook baptizes sixty-three converts at Thuvayoor — establishing the very first Pentecostal church in India. That congregation thrives to this day as a Church of God local church.
1921
Thrikkannamangal Headquarters
Cook establishes his headquarters at Thrikkannamangal and registers his churches under the name Malankara Full Gospel Church of God. The work spreads rapidly throughout Kerala as future leaders join hands.
1922
South India's First Bible School
Due to the urgent need for trained ministers, Cook begins a Home Bible Training School at his residence at Villakonam Bungalow — the first Pentecostal Bible Study centre in all of South India. This institution would later become the internationally accredited Mt. Zion Bible College.
December 1923
First General Convention
The first General Convention of all Pentecostal Churches in India is held on the banks of the river Pampa at Arattupuzha — uniting all Pentecostal missionaries and native ministers across India under one banner.
1936
Merger with Church of God International
During the Church of God's Golden Jubilee World Tour, Rev. J.H. Ingram meets Cook and Indian leaders in Ooty. Cook's organization officially merges with the Church of God International — uniting Kerala's Pentecostal movement with a global family spanning 173 nations.
The Legacy He Left

Foundations That Still Stand

01
First Pentecostal Church

The congregation at Thuvayoor established in January 1914 — the first Pentecostal church in all of India — continues to thrive as a Church of God local church over a century later.

02
Mt. Zion Bible College

The humble home Bible school Cook started in 1922 grew into Mt. Zion Bible College — now holding the 3rd grade level international accreditation of the Church of God Education Department.

03
1,200+ Churches in Kerala

From sixty-three converts at Thuvayoor, the movement Cook began now encompasses over 1,200 local churches and 1,500+ ministers across all of Kerala state.
