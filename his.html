Our Legacy

Our Founder

Rev. Robert Felix Cook
Founder · Missionary Pioneer

Robert F. Cook served as the prime missionary responsible for establishing the Pentecostal movement in India. He came to India in October 1913, and by January 1914, after a four-day revival, baptized sixty-three converts at Thuvayoor — marking the birth of the first Pentecostal church in India.

Cook had received the Pentecostal experience and the anointing of the Holy Spirit at the Upper Room Missions in Los Angeles in 1908. Moved by a stirring message from missionary Rev. George Berg at the 1912 World Pentecostal Camp Meeting, Cook and his wife Anna dedicated themselves to the mission fields of India.

He registered his churches under the name Malankara Full Gospel Church of God. As the work spread, he established the first Home Bible Training School — now the prestigious Mount Zion Bible Seminary — and organized the first General Convention in December 1923 at Arattupuzha.

In 1936, Cook's independent organization merged with the Church of God International. At the time of unification, the Malankara Church counted 63 churches, 43 ministers, and $13,133 worth of properties. Cook was appointed as the first Missionary Overseer of the Church of God in India.

In 1949, Cook returned to the United States. On January 12, 1958, at Cleveland, Tennessee, he passed into glory — leaving behind a legacy that spans more than a century and stretches across the nations of the world.

Global Roots

International Office

Founded: August 19, 1886 · Monroe County, Tennessee, USA

The Church of God began when former Baptist minister Richard Green Spurling preached in a millhouse along Barney Creek. Eight persons formed a Christian Union with the purpose of following the New Testament as their rule for faith and practice, giving equal rights to interpret Scripture.

Twenty-one years later the growing movement formally adopted the name Church of God. Under the leadership of first General Overseer A. J. Tomlinson, the Church adopted a centralized form of government with an inclusive International General Assembly (1906), launched world evangelization beginning in the Bahamas (1909), and established educational opportunities for ministers and members (1918).

Ten years after the founding, a revival at Shearer Schoolhouse in Camp Creek, North Carolina, introduced the doctrine of sanctification. Though persecution followed, a spirit of revival prevailed and Holiness believers experienced an outpouring of the Holy Spirit — including speaking in tongues and divine healing — preparing the way for the global Pentecostal explosion of the early twentieth century.

Kerala Heritage

State Headquarters & History

1913
Cook Arrives in India
Rev. Robert Felix Cook accompanied by Rev. George Berg arrived in India, reaching Kottarakkara in December 1913. Berg handed over his missions to Cook before returning to the US.
1914
First Pentecostal Church Established
After a four-day revival at Thuvayoor, Cook baptized sixty-three converts — marking the founding of the first Pentecostal church in India. This congregation is today the Church of God in India, Thuvayoor Local Church.
1917–1918
Malankara Full Gospel Church Founded
Cook registered his churches as Malankara Full Gospel Church of God at Thrikkannamangal, Kottarakkara. He later married Bertha Fox in 1918, and together they continued the mission work.
1922
First Bible College in South India
Cook established a Home Bible Training School at Villakonam Bungalow, Thrikkannamangal — considered the first Pentecostal Bible study centre in South India. Bertha Cook served as its first principal. Today this is Mount Zion Bible Seminary.
1923
First General Convention
The first general convention was held on the banks of river Pumba at Arattupuzha in December 1923 — the first General Convention of all Pentecostal Churches in India, with all Pentecostal missionaries and native leaders united under Cook's banner.
1926
First Pentecostal Publication
Cook published The Full Gospel Preacher, the first Pentecostal monthly in India, with Cook as editor and Rev. K. E. Abraham as sub-editor.
1936
Merger with Church of God International
Cook's independent organization merged with the Church of God during its Golden Jubilee year through the facilitation of Rev. J. H. Ingram at Mulakuzha. The merged body had 63 churches, 43 ministers, and $13,133 in properties. Cook became the first Missionary Overseer.
1938–1972
Missionary Era
Church of God sent missionaries including Rev. Hoyle Case (1938), Rev. Edward Crick (1943–1948), Rev. C. E. French (1947–1952), and others. In 1972, decentralization policy gave each state autonomous administrative authority.
1964–Present
State Representatives & Overseers
Kerala State received its first State Representative in 1964. The state has been served by ten State Overseers, guiding the church through decades of growth and renewal.

Sacred Practices

Ordinances of the Church

💧

I. Water Baptism

Water Baptism signifies a burial beneath the water and a lifting out again — an immersion that symbolises death to sin and resurrection to new life in Christ.

Baptism is not the door into the church, but an act of obedience following conversion. Certificates of Baptism are issued to those baptized by the officiating minister.

Immersion is recognized as the scriptural mode of baptism. Ministers are recommended to be baptized with the Holy Spirit, though the conscience of the individual is respected.

Water Baptism is administered by Ordained or Licensed Male Ministers in accordance with the commission given by Jesus in Matthew 28:19 — in the Name of the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost.

🍞

II. Lord's Supper & Feet Washing

Both Communion and Feet Washing are taught in the New Testament and may be observed at the same service or at different times, at the option of local churches.

To preserve the unity of the body and to obey the sacred word, it is recommended that every member engage in these sacred services — to be observed one or more times each year.

The Lord's Supper commemorates the sacrifice of Christ and is observed with reverence as a holy remembrance of His body and blood given for our redemption.

Feet Washing, as instituted by our Lord Jesus Christ in John 13, is a sacred act of humility and servanthood, observed as a spiritual ordinance in keeping with His direct command.

Called to Serve

Ministry Credentials

✝
Rank I
Ordained Minister
Qualifications
  • Must meet biblical requirements set forth in 1 Timothy 3:1-7
  • Age 25 with 8 years active ministry, or age 30 with 5 years active ministry
  • Must possess the Baptism of the Holy Ghost
  • Must pass examination in Church government, doctrine & biblical knowledge
  • Wife of applicant must be grave, not a slanderer, sober and faithful
Rights & Authorities
  • Preach, publish, teach and defend the Gospel
  • Serve as Pastor, District or Area Pastor
  • Baptize converts
  • Administer Holy Sacraments
  • Assist in Ordination Ceremonies
  • Establish and organize churches
📜
Rank II
Licensed Minister
Qualifications
  • Must possess the Baptism of the Holy Ghost
  • Actively engaged in pastoral or evangelistic work
  • Thoroughly acquainted with Church of God teachings and doctrines
  • Must pass examination by the Board of Examiners
Rights & Authorities
  • Preach, publish, teach and defend the Gospel
  • Do the work of an Evangelist
  • Serve as Pastor of a Church
  • Baptize converts & receive believers into membership
  • Administer Holy Sacraments
  • Solemnize rites of matrimony
  • Establish churches
May sit in the General Council of Ordained Ministers without voting privileges. Eligible for Ordination at age 25 (8 yrs ministry) or age 30 (5 yrs ministry).
🕊️
Rank III · Entry Level
Exhorter Minister
Qualifications
  • Primary rank — all applicants must first serve as Exhorters (except those from other reputable organizations)
  • Must possess the Baptism of the Holy Ghost
  • Actively engaged in evangelistic or pastoral ministry before recommendation
  • Must pass examination by the Board of Examiners
Rights & Authorities
  • Preach, publish, teach and defend the Gospel
  • Serve as helper, evangelist, or pastor of a church
  • In emergency: authorized by State Overseer to baptize and receive members
  • Solemnize matrimony where state laws recognize Exhorter credentials
  • Receive tithes when available as a serving pastor or helper
Must be active in ministry and pay tithes to the State Overseer. May be promoted to Licensed Minister when deemed qualified.
