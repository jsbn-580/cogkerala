A Global Movement
Born in a Millhouse

The Church of God began on August 19, 1886, when Richard Green Spurling preached in a millhouse along Barney Creek, Tennessee. Eight persons gathered to follow the New Testament as their rule of faith — and that flame has never gone out, spreading now to 173 countries around the world.

The Church of God International, headquartered at Cleveland, Tennessee, USA, stands today as the leading Pentecostal Holiness organisation on earth — and Church of God Kerala is a proud part of this global family.

Origin Story

From a Millhouse
to the World

Former Baptist Richard Green Spurling gathered eight persons at a millhouse along Barney Creek in Monroe County, Tennessee. They formed a Christian Union with one purpose: to follow the New Testament as their rule for faith and practice, giving each other equal rights and privilege to interpret Scripture.

Twenty-one years later the growing movement formally adopted the name Church of God. A revival at the Shearer Schoolhouse in nearby Camp Creek, North Carolina introduced the doctrine of sanctification — and despite fierce persecution, the Holy Spirit outpouring prevailed, including speaking in tongues and divine healing.

Today, from its international headquarters in Cleveland, Tennessee, the Church of God oversees a movement present in 173 countries — making it the leading Pentecostal Holiness organisation in the world.

Kerala's Place in a World Mission

International HQ

Kerala's Connection

Kerala State Church of God is an integral part of the Church of God International, having formally affiliated in 1936. Today Kerala networks with churches in 187 countries.

Kerala State joins the global Church of God family in the FINISH Commitment — fulfilling the Great Commission of Matthew 28:19 to reach every people group on earth.
