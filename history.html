Our Founder

A Mission Trailblazer
for Kerala

The foundation stone of Pentecostalism in India was laid by Rev. R.F. Cook — a man of great missionary zeal. He and his wife Anna received the Pentecostal experience and the anointing of the Holy Spirit at the Upper Room missions in Los Angeles in 1908, igniting a fire that would reach the shores of Kerala.

1908
Rev. Cook and his wife Anna receive the Pentecostal experience and anointing at the Upper Room missions in Los Angeles, California.
1913
Cook moves to Kottarakkara, Kerala, taking over the mission works and establishing the foundation for Pentecostal ministry in South India.
January 1914
After a four-day revival, Cook baptizes sixty-three converts at Thuvayoor — establishing the first Pentecostal church in India, now a Church of God local church.
1921
Cook establishes his headquarters at Thrikkannamangal and registers his churches under the name Malankara Full Gospel Church of God. The work spreads rapidly throughout Kerala.
1922
Establishing the first Home Bible Training School at his residence — the first Pentecostal Bible Study centre in all of South India, later becoming Mt. Zion Bible College.
1936
Cook's organization officially merges with the Church of God International during their Golden Jubilee World Tour, uniting Kerala's Pentecostal movement with a global family.
International Office

A Global Movement
Rooted in Scripture

The Church of God began on August 19, 1886, in Monroe County, Tennessee, near the North Carolina border, when Richard Green Spurling preached in a millhouse along Barney Creek. Eight persons formed a Christian Union to follow the New Testament as their rule of faith and practice — giving each other equal rights to interpret Scripture and sit together as the church of God.

Twenty-one years later, the growing movement formally adopted the name Church of God. Today, its international headquarters stands at Cleveland, Tennessee, USA — overseeing a Holiness Pentecostal movement present in 173 countries around the world, making it the leading Pentecostal Holiness organization on earth.

State Headquarters · Kerala
State Headquarters

Mount Zion, Mulakuzha
Chengannur

The history of the Church of God in India traces back to Rev. Robert Felix Cook and his pioneering Pentecostal organization. The first General Convention of Pentecostal Churches in India was held in December 1923 on the banks of the river Pampa at Arattupuzha — a gathering that united all Pentecostal missionaries and native ministers.

Today, from the State Headquarters at Mount Zion, Mulakuzha, Chengannur, Kerala State Church of God oversees a thriving network that ministers to the people of God across 14 revenue districts and 63 Taluks — proclaiming the love of Christ to all 337 lakh people of Kerala.

First Bible College
In 1922, Rev. Cook began a Home Bible School — the first Pentecostal Bible Study centre in South India. Today it is the internationally accredited Mt. Zion Bible College.
First Convention
December 1923, the first General Convention of all Pentecostal Churches in India convened on the banks of river Pampa at Arattupuzha — a historic gathering.
1,200+ Churches
Kerala State boasts over 1,200 local churches and 1,500+ ministers, spanning all 14 revenue districts and 63 taluks of the state.
First Church · Thuvayoor
The congregation established in January 1914 at Thuvayoor — the very first Pentecostal church in India — continues to thrive as a Church of God local church today.
Core Doctrines

What We Believe

The Church of God stands for the whole Bible rightly divided. The New Testament is our only rule for government and discipline. We hold to the following Declaration of Faith as our standard and official expression of doctrine.

01
The Holy Scriptures

The Church of God believes the whole Bible to be completely and equally inspired — the written Word of God, our supreme guide for faith, practice, and all matters of life.

02
The Holy Trinity

In one God eternally existing in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost — co-equal, co-eternal, and of one divine nature.

03
Jesus Christ

That Jesus Christ is the only begotten Son of the Father, conceived of the Holy Ghost, born of the Virgin Mary — crucified, buried, and raised from the dead.

04
Salvation & New Birth

That all have sinned and that repentance is commanded by God for all people. Justification, regeneration, and the new birth are wrought by faith in the blood of Jesus Christ.

05
Water Baptism

Baptism by immersion — to plunge, dip, or bury beneath the surface of the water and lift out again — as commanded by our Lord Jesus Christ for all believers.

06
Holiness & Spirit

The foundation of our church is built upon the principles of Biblical holiness. We believe in the sanctifying power of the Holy Spirit, with speaking in tongues as the initial sign.
