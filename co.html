Understanding Our Approach

What is Christian Counselling?

Christian counselling focuses on intertwining the disciplines of faith and psychology to provide an approach to mental and emotional health that draws from biblical teachings. Practitioners incorporate religious scripture and teachings to guide you through challenging life issues.

When facing turbulent life events, incorporating and strengthening your faith may be the missing piece in finding proper treatment. Through a personal relationship with God, Christian counselling helps replace emotional instability with self-worth and spiritual fulfilment.

Origins of Christian Counselling

Rooted deep within biblical accounts, this form of therapy places an emphasis on fundamental values and beliefs that comprise the framework of modern Christianity. Ministers and other religious figures must seek licensed training and accreditation. In 1968, Christian counsellors officially formed the Christian Counseling & Educational Foundation — bound not only to religious code but secular standards of ethical practice as well.

What to Expect

Your decision should be based on the credentials and ability of the prospective counsellor. With a Christian counsellor, an additional factor is your personal faith. Finding a counsellor who expresses religious beliefs in an agreeable way, and a willingness to accept the spiritual aspect, is necessary to gain the full benefit.

"Both psychotherapy and counselling describe the same process — both relate to overcoming personal difficulties and working towards positive changes."
What is Spiritual Health?

Christian counsellors understand the importance of mental and emotional health. However, they recognise that traditional methods alone do not provide a comprehensive overview of what may be affecting you. With the additional focus on spiritual health and your relationship with God, personal problems and emotional concerns can be replaced with self-worth and spiritual fulfilment.

"Cast all your anxiety on him because he cares for you." — 1 Peter 5:7
✓Counselling IS:
  • A process where client and counsellor explore difficulties together, including stressful or emotional feelings
  • Helping the client see things more clearly, possibly from a different viewpoint, to facilitate positive change
  • A relationship of trust — confidentiality is paramount and professionally upheld
  • Highlighting the emotional and intellectual experience of a client
✗Counselling is NOT:
  • Giving advice or attempting to sort out the client's problems for them
  • Expecting a client to behave as the counsellor would in a similar situation
  • Getting emotionally involved with the client
  • Looking at problems from the counsellor's own perspective or value system
  • Looking at a client's problems from your own perspective
